Our Certificate & Online Programs For 2021

Elevate your commerce transactions with SC Training (formerly EdApp)’s logistics course. It explores the basics of courier delivery operations, performance assessments, expectation setting, and the best practices for an efficient workflow. It also offers strategies for handling worst-case scenarios. This course lays out the four stages of the work cycle of improvement, and the benefits of modern courier services—from convenience to speed and security. The last module contains a glossary of terms used throughout the course. What sets this logistics course apart is that SC Training uses microlearning, a popular training strategy that drives knowledge retention among your crew. Each concept is broken down into bite-sized chunks with easy-to-follow instructions for better understanding. This course also includes real-life examples and interactive quizzes to promote critical thinking.


Reduce workplace injuries with this manual handling course by SC Training. Since lifting heavy items is part of the daily job, your team could suffer from back and hand injuries if they aren't careful. You can ease their worries with this course as it tackles manual handling risks, lifting techniques, risk elimination, and back protection. This course lists the four steps of manual handling—from hazard identification to risk elimination. It also suggests control measures you can enforce to reduce the risk of injuries and accidents among your team. As an industry-approved course, you don’t have to worry about the quality of your training. Designed with industry experts from SafetyCulture, the lessons here meet the highest standards for training. Using Practical Assessments, you can quickly verify if your team performs their tasks correctly. This in-person assessment tool features a digital checklist that you can customize to your liking. Simply scan each member’s QR code using your mobile phone to access their checklist.


Next on our list of free online logistics courses with certificates is Alison’s supply chain course. If you’re a small business manager looking to introduce your team to the basics of logistics management, you can try this course. It maps out the five stages of a supply chain, its core models, participants, and benchmarking techniques. The course explains the pros and cons of partnering with third and fourth-party logistic providers for your company’s distribution needs. It also includes real-world scenarios to help you assess the current state of your logistics.


This course by Great Learning introduces your team to the basics of logistics management. Here, they’ll learn about logistics network design—from planning terminals and warehouses to selecting ideal locations. Like other distribution training courses, this delves into logistics management categories, logistics elements, and factors affecting the logistics network. Upon finishing the lessons and passing the assessment, your team will receive a completion certificate.
